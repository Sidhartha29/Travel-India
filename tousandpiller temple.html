Thousand Pillar Temple

The Thousand Pillar Temple, located in Warangal, Telangana, is a magnificent 12th-century temple built by the Kakatiya dynasty. It is an architectural marvel, known for its intricately carved pillars, stunning sculptures, and spiritual significance.

This temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva, Vishnu, and Surya, showcasing a blend of Chalukyan and Kakatiyan architecture. The temple stands as a symbol of Telangana's glorious past, featuring intricately carved stone pillars, massive monolithic sculptures, and a grand Nandi statue.

One of the must-visit heritage sites in India, the Thousand Pillar Temple is not just a place of worship but also a masterpiece of ancient Indian craftsmanship and engineering.
