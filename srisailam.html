Srisailam

Srisailam is a significant pilgrimage town located in the Nandyal district of Andhra Pradesh, India. It is home to the Mallikarjuna Jyotirlinga Temple, one of the twelve Jyotirlingas dedicated to Lord Shiva, and is considered one of the most sacred sites for Hindus.

The temple town is situated in the Nallamala Hills along the banks of the Krishna River. It is also famous for the Srisailam Dam, one of the largest hydroelectric projects in India.

Surrounded by dense forests and picturesque landscapes, Srisailam attracts devotees and nature lovers alike. The town is known for its spiritual significance, ancient temples, and stunning views of the Krishna River.
