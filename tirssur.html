Thrissur

History

Thrissur is the administrative capital of Thrissur District, in the central part of Kerala state, India. It is known as the Cultural Capital of Kerala and is famous for the Thrissur Pooram festival.

Thrissur has been politically, economically, and culturally significant throughout history, serving as a gateway for Arabs, Romans, Portuguese, Dutch, and English traders. Christianity, Islam, and Judaism are believed to have first entered India through Thrissur.

Pre-history

Thrissur has a rich prehistoric past, evident from megalithic monuments like menhirs, dolmens, and rock-cut caves found in various parts of the region.
