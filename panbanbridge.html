Pamban Bridge, Rameswaram

The Pamban Bridge is Indiaâ€™s first sea bridge, connecting Rameswaram to mainland Tamil Nadu. Opened in 1914, this engineering marvel stretches across the Palk Strait, offering breathtaking views of the Bay of Bengal.

This double-leaf bascule bridge allows ships to pass underneath, making it one of India's most unique railway bridges. The structure, spanning 2.3 km, is a lifeline for travel between the island and the mainland.

The scenic train journey across the Pamban Bridge is a must-experience for travelers, offering unmatched views of the endless ocean and fishing boats below.
