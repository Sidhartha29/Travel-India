The Ajanta Caves are 30 rock-cut Buddhist cave monuments dating from the second century BCE to about 480 CE in Aurangabad district of Maharashtra, India. Ajanta Caves are a UNESCO World Heritage Site and are regarded as masterpieces of Buddhist religious art.

The caves include paintings and rock-cut sculptures that are among the finest surviving examples of ancient Indian art. They feature monasteries and worship halls, and were a monsoon retreat for monks and a stopover for ancient travelers.
