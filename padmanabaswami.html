Padmanabhaswamy Temple

History

The Padmanabhaswamy Temple is steeped in rich heritage, being referenced in ancient Hindu scriptures like the Vishnu Purana, Brahma Purana, and Mahabharata. It was once known as “The Golden Temple” due to its immense wealth.

Sangam literature and devotional Tamil poetry from saints such as Nammalwar celebrate the temple and the city as radiant with gold. It’s one of the 108 Divya Desams—sacred Vishnu temples glorified in Vaishnavism.

The temple’s legend traces back to Parasurama, who is believed to have installed the idol and handed over its administration to seven Potti families. Its rituals and traditions are rooted in the ancient Brahmanda Purana.
