Gandikota is a village and historical fort on the right bank of the Penna River, in the Kadapa district of Andhra Pradesh, India. It is often referred to as the 'Grand Canyon of India' due to its stunning gorge formed by the river.

The fort was built during the 13th century and played a significant role in the history of various South Indian dynasties, including the Kakatiyas, Vijayanagar Empire, and the Qutb Shahis.

Gandikota is famous for its breathtaking landscapes, the ancient fort, temples, and the picturesque view of the Penna River cutting through rocky terrain. It is a popular tourist destination for history buffs and nature enthusiasts alike.
