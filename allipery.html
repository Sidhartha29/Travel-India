Known as the "Venice of the East," Alleppey is a charming town in Kerala famous for its serene backwaters, lush greenery, and houseboat cruises.

The region of Kuttanad, known as the rice bowl of Kerala, has been historically significant since the Sangam period. Christianity also has deep roots in the area, with St. Thomas, a disciple of Jesus, establishing churches in the first century AD.
