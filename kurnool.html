Kurnool

Kurnool is a historic city in Andhra Pradesh, India, known for its rich heritage and ancient monuments. It served as the first capital of Andhra Pradesh from 1953 to 1956 and is an important cultural and economic hub.

The city is home to several historical landmarks, including the famous Konda Reddy Fort, which stands as a symbol of Kurnool's past. Other significant attractions include the Belum Caves, Oravakallu Rock Garden, and the famous temples such as the Yaganti Temple.

Situated along the Tungabhadra River, Kurnool is a blend of history, spirituality, and natural beauty, making it a must-visit destination for travelers and history enthusiasts alike.
