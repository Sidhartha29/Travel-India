The Godavari is the second-longest river in India after the Ganges, flowing through central and southern India. It originates from Trimbak in Maharashtra and traverses several states before emptying into the Bay of Bengal.

The river is often referred to as the 'Dakshina Ganga' (Ganges of the South) due to its immense cultural and religious significance. It supports agriculture, hydroelectric projects, and provides water to millions of people.

Famous attractions along the Godavari include Rajahmundry's historic bridges, the scenic Papikondalu hills, and the ancient temples along its banks, making it a lifeline for both nature and spirituality.
