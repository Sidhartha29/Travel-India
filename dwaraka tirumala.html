About Sri Venkateswara Temple

Sri Venkateswara Temple, located in Dwaraka Tirumala in Andhra Pradesh, is dedicated to Lord Venkateswara, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu. The temple is often referred to as 'Chinna Tirupati' due to its resemblance to the famous Tirupati temple. It attracts millions of pilgrims every year, making it a major spiritual hub.

History of the Temple

The temple's history traces back to the sage Dwaraka, who discovered the self-manifested idol after years of penance. Over time, several rulers contributed to the temple's grandeur, including Dharma Appa Rao, who constructed many of the key structures.

Significance

Known for its unique idol placement and serene surroundings, the temple offers a peaceful atmosphere for devotees seeking spiritual solace. The temple's architecture, which includes the vimana, mantapa, and gopura, enhances its historical and cultural significance.
